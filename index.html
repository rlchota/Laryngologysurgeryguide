Airway Surgery Procedures

Airway Dilation

▼

Opening up your airway by stretching it with a balloon

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, steroid injection, lysis of adhesion and balloon dilation

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and airway. We give you a steroid shot in the scar tissue, make small cuts to loosen things up, and then use a balloon to stretch your airway open.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice might sound different, but this should go away quickly. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store if you need pain relief. You might feel tired or sleepy from the medicine on the day of surgery, but you can usually go back to your normal activities the day after.

Cordotomy

▼

Taking out part of your vocal cord to help you breathe better

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, cordotomy/arytenoidectomy

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and airway. We remove a piece of your vocal cord to open your airway. Sometimes we also give a steroid shot to help healing or use a balloon to stretch your airway.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice will sound different. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store, and your doctor might give you stronger pain medicine if needed.

You will probably stay in the hospital one night so we can make sure you are healing well. We will also watch to make sure swallowing is okay. Once you leave the hospital, you can go back to normal activities in a few days when you feel ready.

Long Term Changes

Your voice will change permanently after this surgery. Your voice may sound more breathy and weaker. This is normal and expected—better breathing is worth the voice change.

Tracheostomy

▼

Making a breathing hole in your neck with a special tube (also called a "trach")

Medical Name

Tracheostomy

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we make a cut in the front of your neck and create a hole into your windpipe. We then put in a trach tube—this is a plastic tube you will breathe through.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You will stay in the hospital for several days. The trach tube will be stitched to your neck at first to help it heal and stay in place. After a few days, we will change your trach tube, and then it's usually safe to go home.

It may be hard to talk and swallow at first, but we can give you a special valve to help you talk. Our nurses will teach you how to take care of your trach and make sure you have supplies to take home. You will learn how to keep it clean and safe.

Voice Surgery Procedures

Microflap Excision

▼

Removing a growth or bump on your vocal cord

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, microflap excision of lesion

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and airway. Using a microscope to see really close, we carefully remove the growth on your vocal cord. We might fix your vocal cord using a special technique called a microflap.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice might sound different, but this should go away quickly. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store if you need pain relief. You might feel tired or sleepy from the medicine on the day of surgery, but you can usually do most normal activities the day after.

Voice Rest - Very Important!

This is the hardest part, but very important for healing. Your doctor may put you on "voice rest":

  • NO talking for about 4 days after surgery
  • NO whispering, humming, or making any sounds
  • After 4 days, slowly start talking again over 1-2 weeks
  • Don't yell or sing for 6 weeks
  • Your doctor will tell you exactly how long

Restylane Injection

▼

Injecting filler into your vocal cord to help your voice

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, injection laryngoplasty

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and airway. We inject a filler called Restylane into your vocal cord. This makes your vocal cord bigger and helps you talk better.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice might sound different, but this should go away quickly. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store if you need pain relief. You might feel tired or sleepy from the medicine on the day of surgery, but you can usually do normal activities the day after.

Your voice should sound better right away, though it might feel tight or strange for a few days. It should get better after a few days.

How long it lasts: The filler lasts about 12 weeks (3 months). After that, the filler goes away and you might need another injection later.

Swallow Surgery Procedures

Balloon Dilation

▼

Stretching the top of your food tube (esophagus) with a balloon

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, rigid esophagoscopy with balloon dilation

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and the start of your food tube (esophagus). We then put a balloon in the top part of your food tube and blow it up to stretch it open.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice might sound different, but this should go away quickly. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store if you need pain relief. You might feel tired or sleepy from the medicine on the day of surgery, but you can usually go back to your normal activities the day after, including eating your normal food.

Cricopharyngeal Botox

▼

Botox shot in the muscle at the top of your food tube to help swallowing

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, rigid esophagoscopy with balloon dilation and botox injection

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and the start of your food tube (esophagus). We give a Botox shot into the muscle at the top of your food tube. We often use a balloon to stretch it too.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You may have a sore throat and your voice might sound different, but this should go away quickly. You can take Tylenol or Ibuprofen from the store if you need pain relief. You might feel tired or sleepy from the medicine on the day of surgery, but you can usually go back to your normal activities the day after, including eating your normal food.

When will I feel better? The Botox starts working in about 48 hours (2 days), and you should notice swallowing is easier.

Cricopharyngeal Myotomy

▼

Using a laser to cut the muscle at the top of your food tube

Medical Name

Direct laryngoscopy, cricopharyngeal myotomy

What We Do During Surgery

After you are asleep, we put a special camera scope in your mouth to look at your voice box and the start of your food tube (esophagus). We use a laser to cut the muscle at the top of your food tube. This helps food go down easier.

Taking Care of Yourself After Surgery

You will stay in the hospital so we can watch you. We might put in a feeding tube during surgery to help you get food while you heal. You cannot eat or drink the night of surgery.

The next morning we do a swallow test to check if there are any leaks in your throat:

  • If there is NO leak: You can start drinking liquids. If you do well, you can go home.
  • If there IS a leak: You will need to stay longer in the hospital with the feeding tube. This gives your throat time to heal.

You Need to Stay in the Hospital

This surgery needs a hospital stay. We need to do a swallow test before you eat to keep you safe.
